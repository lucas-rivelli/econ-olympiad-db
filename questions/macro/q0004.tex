\question{
  id        = {0004},
  title     = {1+1=2?},
  source    = {OBECON},
  year      = {2026},
  round     = {Seletiva IEO -- Phase A},
  language  = {English},
  topic     = {Macro},
  subtopic  = {Labor Markets},
  type      = {objective},
  statement = {%
    Consider the following news headline regarding Immigration Policy in the
    United States:

    \begin{center}
      \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{../images/q0004_fig1.png}
    \end{center}

    How will the effect of the current U.S.\ administration's proposed changes
    to the H-1B visa policy impact the welfare of the U.S workforce?
  },
  choices   = {%
    \item The result depends on the complementarity or substitutability of the
          foreign workers with American workers. If foreign workers are
          complements, the changes will decrease the welfare of the U.S
          workforce. If they are substitutes, the changes will increase the
          welfare of the U.S workforce.
    \item The changes will increase the welfare of the U.S workforce as more
          jobs will be available for American citizens, leading to lower
          competition for jobs and consequently higher wages.
    \item The changes will decrease the welfare of the U.S workforce as it
          will attract more skilled workers from abroad, due to the possibility
          of earning more than \$100,000, leading to higher competition for
          jobs and consequently lower wages.
    \item The changes will have no effect on the welfare of the U.S workforce
          as the number of H1B visas issued is relatively small compared to
          the total workforce, leading to an irrelevant impact on job
          availability and wages.
    \item The changes will increase the welfare of the U.S workforce, as new
          jobs will open only for American citizens, but the overall effect on
          wages will be negligible due to the high demand for skilled labor
          in the U.S.
  },
  answer    = {A},
  solution  = {%
    The key insight is that the effect of restricting skilled foreign labor
    depends on whether those workers are \emph{complements} or
    \emph{substitutes} to American workers.

    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Substitutes:} Foreign workers compete directly with
            Americans for the same jobs. Restricting them reduces labor supply
            in that segment, raising wages for Americans~$\Rightarrow$
            welfare \textbf{increases}.
      \item \textbf{Complements:} Foreign skilled workers boost American
            workers' productivity (e.g.\ a foreign software engineer enables
            more American project managers). Restricting them reduces
            productivity and wages for Americans~$\Rightarrow$ welfare
            \textbf{decreases}.
    \end{itemize}

    Since the question cannot be answered without knowing the relationship,
    \textbf{(A)} is the only correct alternative.
  },
}
