  language  = {English},
  topic     = {Macro},
  subtopic  = {Monetary Policy / Money Multiplier},
  type      = {numeric},
  statement = {%
    A fractional-reserve banking system has a required reserve ratio of 20\%.
    The central bank injects \$400 million of new reserves via open market
    operations. Banks lend all excess reserves; no currency leakages.
    Households' MPC = 0.6.

    Calculate the total change in GDP (in \$ millions). Round to nearest
    integer; if 0.5, round to even.
  },
  choices   = {},
  answer    = {5000},
  solution  = {%
    \textbf{Step 1 --- Money multiplier:}
    \[
      m = \frac{1}{\text{reserve ratio}} = \frac{1}{0.20} = 5
    \]
    New money supply $= \$400\text{M} \times 5 = \$2{,}000\text{M}$.

    \textbf{Step 2 --- Spending/fiscal multiplier:}
    \[
      k = \frac{1}{1 - \text{MPC}} = \frac{1}{1 - 0.6} = \frac{1}{0.4} = 2.5
    \]

    \textbf{Step 3 --- Total change in GDP:}
    \[
      \Delta Y = 2{,}000 \times 2.5 = \mathbf{\$5{,}000\text{ million}}
    \]

    The biblical reference (Matthew 14: feeding 5,000 with a few loaves)
    alludes to the multiplier effect ``multiplying'' the initial injection.
  },
}
