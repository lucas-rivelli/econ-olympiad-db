  round     = {Seletiva IEO -- Phase C},
  language  = {English},
  topic     = {Macro},
  subtopic  = {Supply Shocks / Stagflation},
  type      = {objective},
  statement = {%
    Braeconomia is a major agricultural-exporting country responsible for 82\%
    of the galaxy's food exports (40\% of galaxy's GDP). In December 2030, it
    ceased all exports for 6 months. What was the primary economic effect on
    interplanetary markets?
  },
  choices   = {%
    \item Importing nations immediately began producing their own food, given
          variable capital.
    \item The interplanetary market suffered a major supply shock, increasing
          price levels and unemployment.
    \item The interplanetary market suffered a major demand shock, decreasing
          price levels and unemployment.
    \item There was no significant effect on interplanetary markets.
  },
  answer    = {B},
  solution  = {%
    A sudden halt in 82\% of food supply constitutes a large negative supply
    shock: less output is available at any given price level, causing prices to
    rise and production to fall, resulting in stagflation. (B) is correct.
  },
}
