  subtopic  = {Monetary Policy / Central Banking},
  type      = {objective},
  statement = {%
    Consider four statements about monetary policy and central banks. Which are
    correct?\\[4pt]
    \textbf{I.}~Central banks use reserve requirements as the primary
    inflation-control tool, increasing ratios during expansions.\\
    \textbf{II.}~Quantitative easing involves large-scale purchases of
    government or corporate securities when conventional policy fails at the
    zero lower bound.\\
    \textbf{III.}~To lower interest rates, central banks purchase government
    bonds in open market operations, injecting liquidity.\\
    \textbf{IV.}~The Federal Reserve has a dual mandate (price stability and
    maximum employment); the Central Bank of Brazil has a single mandate
    (inflation targeting).
  },
  choices   = {%
    \item Only I and III.
    \item Only I and IV.
    \item Only II, III and IV.
    \item Only II and III.
  },
  answer    = {C},
  solution  = {%
    Statement~I is false: policy interest rates (not reserve requirements) are
    the primary modern tool for controlling inflation. Statements~II, III,
    and~IV are all accurate descriptions of modern monetary policy practice.
    (C) is correct.
  },
}
