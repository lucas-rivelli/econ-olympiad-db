  type      = {objective},
  statement = {%
    Ricardo holds an equity portfolio highly correlated with the stock market.
    He evaluates two funds: Fund Equity (high returns, invests in stocks) and
    Fund Bond (moderate returns, invests in bonds). Which should he choose to
    best diversify his portfolio?
  },
  choices   = {%
    \item Equity Fund, since it offers higher returns with low systematic risk.
    \item Equity Fund, because additional equity exposure won't significantly
          increase overall risk.
    \item Bond Fund, as it provides diversification and high yields.
    \item Bond Fund, because it offers diversification together with moderate
          returns.
  },
  answer    = {D},
  solution  = {%
    Ricardo's portfolio is already heavily concentrated in equities. Fund Bond
    provides diversification through low (or negative) correlation with stocks.
    Modern portfolio theory shows that combining asset classes with low
    correlations reduces overall portfolio risk while maintaining acceptable
    returns. (D) is correct.
  },
}
