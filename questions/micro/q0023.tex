  id        = {0023},
  title     = {Robin Hood},
  source    = {OBECON},
  year      = {2026},
  round     = {Seletiva IEO -- Phase A},
  language  = {English},
  topic     = {Micro},
  subtopic  = {Income Distribution / Poverty},
  type      = {numeric},
  statement = {%
    Consider the following income distribution for a small economy with 8
    individuals:

    \vspace{6pt}
    \begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{lc}
      \toprule
      \textbf{Individual} & \textbf{Annual Income (\$)} \\
      \midrule
      Alice   & 3,000  \\
      Bob     & 4,000  \\
      Carlos  & 5,000  \\
      Diana   & 9,000  \\
      Eva     & 21,000 \\
      Felipe  & 25,000 \\
      Gabriel & 35,000 \\
      Helena  & 64,000 \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
    \vspace{6pt}

    The government taxes the top 25\% earners \$10,000 each and distributes
    the total equally among the bottom 50\% earners. The poverty line is 50\%
    of the \emph{status quo} median income. By what percentage does this policy
    reduce the number of people in poverty?

    Median of $a_1,\ldots,a_{2n}$ is $\dfrac{a_n+a_{n+1}}{2}$. Round to
    nearest integer; if fractional part is 0.5, round to nearest even integer.
  },
  choices   = {},
  answer    = {100},
  solution  = {%
    \textbf{Step 1 --- Status quo median and poverty line:}\\
    Sorted incomes: 3k, 4k, 5k, 9k, 21k, 25k, 35k, 64k ($n=4$).\\
    Median $= \frac{a_4 + a_5}{2} = \frac{9{,}000 + 21{,}000}{2} = \$15{,}000$.\\
    Poverty line $= 50\% \times 15{,}000 = \$7{,}500$.

    \textbf{Step 2 --- People in poverty before policy:}\\
    Alice (\$3k), Bob (\$4k), Carlos (\$5k): all $< \$7{,}500$. \textbf{3 people.}

    \textbf{Step 3 --- Apply policy:}\\
    Top 25\% = top 2 earners: Gabriel and Helena, each taxed \$10,000.\\
    Total transferred $= \$20{,}000$, split equally among bottom 50\% (4
    people): each receives $\$5{,}000$.\\
    New incomes:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Alice: $3{,}000 + 5{,}000 = \$8{,}000 > \$7{,}500$ \\
      \item Bob: $4{,}000 + 5{,}000 = \$9{,}000 > \$7{,}500$ \\
      \item Carlos: $5{,}000 + 5{,}000 = \$10{,}000 > \$7{,}500$ \\
      \item Diana: $9{,}000 + 5{,}000 = \$14{,}000 > \$7{,}500$
    \end{itemize}

    \textbf{Step 4 --- People in poverty after policy:} \textbf{0 people.}

    \textbf{Reduction:} $\dfrac{3 - 0}{3} \times 100 = \mathbf{100\%}$.
  },
}
